Important people of the city

Rosalía de Castro

Rosalía de Castro was born in Santiago de Compostela on February 23, 1837. The date of 1836 that appears on her baptism
certificate is an error by the chaplain, since all the certificates that precede and follow are from the year 1837. In a document
signed before a notary public in 1843, Doña Teresa de Castro recognizes Rosalía as her natural daughter and gives the date of
birth as February 23, 1837. The document was with the papers from the wedding file of Murguía and Rosalía. Murguía had
requested exemption from fees, due to lack of financial resources, and the Church had filed it in the POOR section where no
one had thought to look for it. It was found there in 2021 by the researcher Sagrario Abelleira and the archivist Irene Galindo.

Camilo José Cela

Camilo José Cela Trulock. (Iria Flavia, A Coruña, May 11, 1916 - Madrid, January 17, 2002). Spanish writer and academic, winner
of the Nobel Prize for Literature.
In 1925 his family moved to Madrid. Before completing his high school studies, he fell ill and was interned in a sanatorium in
Guadarrama (Madrid) during 1931 and 1932, where he used forced rest in long reading sessions.
