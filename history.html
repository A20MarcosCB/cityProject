History of Padrón

The name

Padrón is the first place that appears in the Jacobean legend. In the historic center of Padrón, in addition to the rúas, the "Pedrón" of which legend speaks, in which the Apostle's boat moored, the Convent of Carmen, with a viewpoint over the town, and the house of the Romantic poet Rosalía de Castro. The route is marked by two rivers, the Sar and the Ulla, and by its friendly and productive plains, where the well-known Padrón peppers are born.
